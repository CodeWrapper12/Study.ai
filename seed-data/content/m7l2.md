# The Mock Loop Playbook

Knowledge doesn't fail interviews; execution does. The final two weeks are deliberate practice under realistic conditions.

## The weekly rhythm (weeks 14–15)
- 2 timed DSA sessions (2 problems / 45 min, talking aloud the whole time)
- 1 full 45-min system design mock (use the M2L5 prompt, then a fresh one: chat system, notification platform, file storage)
- 1 behavioral mock (random 5 questions against your story bank)
- Mock sources: a peer/colleague, interviewing.io or Pramp-style platforms, or run them solo with recordings — a real human for at least two of them.

## In-round execution rules
- **Coding:** restate → clarify constraints → brute force aloud → optimize → code → trace one example + one edge. If stuck: *"Let me think about what data structure gives me O(1) here..."* — silence is the only unrecoverable error.
- **System design:** drive the structure yourself (the M2L1 sequence); check in every few minutes — "want me to go deeper on the matching service or move to data?" Interviewers reward candidates who manage the 45 minutes like a meeting they're chairing — which, as the senior in the room, you've done for years.
- **Behavioral:** answer the question asked, 90 seconds, stop talking. Rambling past 2.5 minutes is the most common senior failure.

## Pipeline sequencing
Order applications in three tiers: warm-up companies (weeks 1–2 of applying), target locals (Careem, Talabat, banks, consultancies), dream loops (Microsoft Dubai, Amazon) last — your tenth interview is dramatically better than your first. Never burn a dream company as practice.

## Lab
Schedule (calendar invites, not intentions) all six mocks across the two weeks. After each: 3-line retro — what broke, what to drill, what improved.
