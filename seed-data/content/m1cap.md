# Module 1 Capstone: The Simulated Screen

This is a timed simulation of a real coding screen, graded against the rubric below — the same dimensions actual interviewers score.

## Format

Block out **90 minutes, uninterrupted**. Camera or voice recorder ON (you will hate this; it is the point — reviewing your own narration is the fastest communication upgrade available).

Solve these three problems **in order, speaking aloud the entire time**, in a blank editor with no autocomplete, no docs, no AI:

1. **Group Anagrams** (target: 15 min) — hashing + canonicalization
2. **Daily Temperatures** (target: 20 min) — monotonic stack with the amortized argument narrated
3. **Course Schedule** (target: 25 min) — graph modeling + topological sort

The remaining ~30 minutes: re-watch/listen to your recording and write the self-review (below).

## Rules

- Narrate constantly: restate, clarify constraints aloud (even rhetorically), brute force first, name the pattern, state complexity before and after coding.
- Dry-run each solution on one normal case and one edge case, out loud, line by line.
- If stuck > 10 minutes on one problem, note it, move on, return at the end — exactly as you should in a real screen.

## Submit

1. Your three solutions (paste code or a repo/gist link).
2. A self-review (300–500 words): where your narration went silent, which dry-run caught a bug (or failed to), per-problem timing vs targets, and the two specific behaviors you will change before the next simulation.
3. Your grind tracker totals for the module: problems solved per pattern, clean-solve rate, and your current +3/+7/+21 review queue size.

## Grading rubric (100 points)

- **Correctness (30):** all three solutions produce correct results on the standard cases, including edges.
- **Pattern recognition & approach (20):** named the right pattern quickly; justified it; brute force stated first.
- **Communication (20):** continuous narration; complexity stated unprompted; trade-offs voiced (judged from your self-review's honesty and specificity).
- **Code quality (15):** idiomatic Python from Module 1 — Counter/defaultdict/deque where they belong, no O(n²) string building, clean naming.
- **Self-assessment & system (15):** self-review identifies real, specific behaviors (not "be more confident"); tracker shows the spaced-repetition system actually running.

**Pass ≥ 60.** Honest expectation: most first attempts land 55–75. A 60 with a brutally honest self-review beats an 85 with a vague one — the rubric rewards the habit that compounds.
