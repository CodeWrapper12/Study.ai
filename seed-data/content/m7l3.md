# Gulf & Big-Tech Compensation: Levels and Negotiation

You're switching for pay and scale — so treat the offer stage as its own engineering problem. Most candidates prepare 100 hours for interviews and 0 for the 10-minute conversation that sets 30–40% of the outcome.

## Know the structures
- **Gulf market:** base + housing/transport allowances (often 20–40% on top), annual bonus, end-of-service gratuity. Tax-free base makes raw numbers non-comparable to Western packages — compare *monthly savings potential*, not headline figures.
- **Big tech:** base + RSUs (vesting 4 years, sometimes back-loaded) + signing bonus + annual bonus. Evaluate year-1 and year-4 totals separately; refreshers are not guaranteed — ask.
- **Levels matter more than offers:** Microsoft 63/64 vs 65, Amazon SDE II vs III — the level sets your band for years. If interviews trend senior, you can ask the recruiter what level you're being considered for and make the case for the higher one *before* the loop, not after.

## Negotiation mechanics
- **Never give the first number.** "I'm focused on fit; what range is budgeted for this level?" works in both markets. If forced, give a researched range anchored high (sources below).
- **Competing offers are the only lever that reliably moves big-tech numbers.** This is why pipeline sequencing (M7L2) matters: offers should land within the same 2–3 week window.
- **Everything has a second lever:** signing bonus moves more easily than base; extra RSUs more easily than level; start date and remote days are free wins. Always get the final package in writing before resigning.

## Research sources
levels.fyi (big tech, increasingly Gulf coverage), Glassdoor/Bayt for local bands, recruiter conversations (ask every recruiter for the band — many tell you), peers in your network.

## Lab
Build your numbers sheet: current total comp (everything), minimum acceptable, target, and dream — each justified. Script and rehearse aloud your range-deflection answer and your "competing offer" sentence. Negotiation scripts that exist only in your head don't survive contact.
