# Landing Zones, Identity & UAE Compliance (ISR / DESC / UAE PASS)

This is your **local differentiator**: plenty of candidates know AKS; few can speak Dubai's regulatory language. Your gap analysis flagged ISR v3.1, DESC, and UAE PASS — this lesson gives you the working vocabulary.

## Landing zones
An enterprise-scale landing zone is the pre-governed foundation workloads deploy into: management-group hierarchy, identity (Entra ID), hub-spoke or vWAN networking, Azure Policy guardrails, centralized logging. The interview line: *"Workload teams get a paved road — policy makes the secure path the easy path."* Terraform/Bicep modules for landing zones exist (Azure Landing Zones accelerator); you configure rather than invent.

## Zero Trust in practice (not the slogan)
- Identity is the perimeter: Entra ID everywhere, managed identities for service-to-service (no connection-string credentials — your Key Vault habit extends here).
- Verify explicitly: Conditional Access, MFA, device compliance.
- Least privilege: PIM for just-in-time admin, RBAC scoped to resource groups not subscriptions.
- Assume breach: private endpoints (no public DB endpoints — a DESC favorite), NSG segmentation, Defender for Cloud.

## Dubai-specific layer
- **ISR (Information Security Regulation)** — Dubai government's control framework; government-adjacent projects are audited against it. Practical implications: data classification, in-country residency (UAE North/Central), logging retention, encryption at rest and transit.
- **DESC (Dubai Electronic Security Center)** — the authority issuing/auditing those standards, including cloud service provider certification for government workloads.
- **UAE PASS** — the national digital identity; OIDC-based citizen/resident authentication. Architecturally: standard OIDC integration with a government IdP, plus its signing capability for documents. If you've wired Keycloak, you can wire UAE PASS — say exactly that in interviews.
